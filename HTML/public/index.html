Modeling and Mapping Eviction Rates in California

Our goal is to visualize predicted eviction risk for housing in California.

View Map

The Eviction Process

Notice

Landlord gives 3 - 60 days' written notice

Filing

Landlord files a complaint legally, notifies sherriff's department

Removal

Sheriff has tenant forcefully evicted

Motivation

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, a rent moratorium was set in place in California to prevent mass evictions. The moratorium is set to expire on September 30th, 2021.
Tract-level eviction effects of the moratorium lifting are unclear.

Eviction data is sparse.

1/3 of U.S. counties don’t have annual eviction data.

In California specifically, accurate eviction data is also sparse & unvalidated. The danger of not knowing tract-level eviction rates is creating policy that may harm, ignore, and underserve high risk neighborhoods.

Our goal:

Predict eviction rates for each tract in California.

We sourced validated eviction data on 16 metros nationwide from the renonwed U.C. Berkeley Urban Displacement Project. Utilizing cutting-edge machine learning techniques, we constructed a model to generate predictions over California metros.



Caveats

Conservative Estimates

Since the data we have is only on the last step of the pipeline (rate of legally documented, forced evictions), all of our estimates are likely underestimates of the true number of residents made to leave their homes.

Variation in Accuracy

Our training data is from 16 cities (dense populations). Thus, the model makes more accurate predictions on denser California cities and less accurate predictions on suburbs or rural regions.

2019 Estimates

The predicted values on the map are based on 2019 data, which we don’t claim are fully reflective of the current year.
